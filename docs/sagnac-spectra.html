Sagnac Spectra

Left panel (PSD): A power spectral density (PSD) is computed for hourly data of the Sagnac signal (sinusoidal, 5000 sps) for the previous day. The x‑axis is narrowed around the expected Sagnac beat frequency (ring‑specific). The black curve shows the median over all hourly spectra. Peaks at overtones of the 50 Hz electric grid (e.g. 300, 450, 500 Hz, etc.) are common; other narrow spikes typically indicate electromagnetic interference among RF excitation systems.

Right panel (Helicorder): The 24‑hour helicorder plot displays processed rotation rate data. Each hourly trace is normalized and colour‑coded by hour. Spikes or glitches usually coincide with mode jumps of the counter‑propagating beams. Periods of elevated broadband noise or flat behaviour often correspond to loss of a stable Sagnac interferogram (no valid rotation data).
